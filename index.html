The Macro Tailwind

Assam is Booming.
Its Freight System Isn't.

Assam is India's single fastest-growing major state economy, but its freight system is bottlenecked. The Brahmaputra River sits idle as a freight superhighway.

  • Massive Capital Influx: State GSDP grew 45% (FY20–FY25). Advantage Assam 2.0 attracted ₹5.18 Lakh Crore in investment pledges.
  • Road & Rail Near Capacity: Every tonne of industrial cargo must travel the congested Kolkata–Guwahati corridor. Road haulage is heavily inflated at ~₹3,000 per tonne for bulk commodities.
  • No Local Infrastructure: There is no commercial shipbuilding infrastructure anywhere in the Northeast. Existing vessels must be procured and serviced 2,500+ km away.
The Solution

One Integrated Platform
to Unlock It All

This project builds the missing third option: a shipyard that manufactures the fleet, and a dedicated liner service that operates it.

Component 01: Greenfield Shipyard

A 10-acre riverfront manufacturing yard capable of building vessels up to 5,000 DWT. Equipped with robotic panel lines. India's only commercial shipyard in the Northeast by a margin of 2,500 km.

Component 02: Captive Hybrid Liner

40 hybrid-propulsion (biodiesel-electric) container vessels, purpose-built for year-round NW-2 operations. Routes connect Guwahati to Kolkata, Dhubri to Bangladesh, and Haldia to Myanmar.

~67% Reduction Freight Cost per Tonne

Slashes transport costs from ~₹3,000 by road to ~₹1,000 by waterway.

1 MTPA Cargo Capacity Target

Serving cement, steel, FMCG, and agri clients within 4 years.

Uncontested Absolute Monopoly

Zero private competition currently operating on NW-2. Sets the tariffs & rules.

The Moat

State Backed & Operationally Proven

Promoter Pedigree

  • 30+ Years Experience: Active operator of ports, shipyards, and logistics infrastructure across 4 countries with 650+ employees.
  • Already on NW-2: The group currently manages the Pandu and Dhubri Terminals on NW-2 under IWAI. We already control the terminal infrastructure, the labour ecosystem, and the anchor cargo relationships.
